# Rate Card & Day-Rate Worksheet (2026)

All figures are starting anchors for Western markets, expressed loosely (USD/GBP/EUR land in a similar order of magnitude). Localize before quoting.

## Day-rate worksheet

```
A. Target take-home income (the salary you want)        ________
B. Annual business costs:
   Software subscriptions (Adobe, plugins, render)      ________
   Hardware amortised (machine, drives, monitor)        ________
   Workspace / rent / utilities                         ________
   Insurance + accountancy + legal                      ________
   Pension / retirement set-aside                        ________
   Marketing / portfolio / training                     ________
   Tax set-aside buffer                                  ________
   B TOTAL                                               ________

C. Revenue needed (A + B)                                ________

D. Billable days/year (realistic): typically 120-150     ________

DAY RATE = C ÷ D  =                                       ________
   → round UP to a clean figure.
```

Why ~130 and not 260: weekdays minus holiday (~25), public holidays (~8),
sick/admin/marketing (~40), and unsold gaps (~50+). Pitching at 260 billable
days produces a rate that cannot cover a normal year.
