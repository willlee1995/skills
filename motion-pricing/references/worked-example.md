# Worked Estimate Example

## Brief

1× 30s flat-2D explainer (16:9) + 3× 6s vertical cutdowns (9:16) + 1 static end-frame. Client-supplied script. Brand assets exist. Organic social use, 12 months. Standard timeline. Day rate = £600.

## Step 1 — model

Estimate in days; present as fixed per-project with a 2-round revision cap.

## Step 2 — day estimate by driver

| Phase | Reasoning (drivers) | Days |
|---|---|---|
| Concept & direction | Brief is clear; light concept | 1 |
| Storyboard / animatic | 30s flat 2D, ~8 scenes | 2 |
| Design & illustration | Brand exists; build scene assets | 3 |
| Animation (hero 30s) | Flat 2D, moderate complexity | 5 |
| Cutdowns (3× 6s) | Reuse hero assets, reframe to 9:16 | 1.5 |
| Sound design / music | Light SFX + licensed track sync | 1 |
| Revisions (2 rounds) | ~15% of animation effort | 1.5 |
| Project management | Comms, delivery, QC | 1 |
| **Total** | | **16 days** |

## Step 3 — money

- Production: 16 days × £600 = **£9,600**.
- Rush: none (standard timeline) → +0%.
- Usage uplift: organic social only → base, +0%.
- Contingency: brief is clear, hold at 0; would add 10% if vague.
- **Total (excl. tax): £9,600.**

## Step 4 — cross-check (per-finished-minute)

Finished runtime: 30s + (3×6s) = 48s ≈ 0.8 min.
£9,600 ÷ 0.8 = £12,000/finished minute.
Against the flat-2D explainer range (1,000-3,000/min) this looks high per-minute — but the per-minute metric penalises short-form, where fixed concept/design costs do not shrink with runtime. The day-based number is the honest one; the per-minute check just flags that short films cost more per second. Quote stands on the day math.

## Step 5 — present

Show the client the fixed £9,600 with the 2-round cap and the exclusions, NOT the day breakdown line-by-line (keep day math internal unless billing by day).

## Variant: same job, broadcast + paid, rush

- Usage uplift for paid + broadcast: +75% → £9,600 × 1.75 = £16,800.
- Rush (timeline halved): +40% on production base → +£3,840.
- New total ≈ £20,640 (excl. tax).

Same animation, very different price — because usage and rush are real cost/value drivers, not padding.
