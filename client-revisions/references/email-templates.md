# Email Templates

## 1. Consolidated-feedback request

```
Subject: Feedback on [project] — Round [N]

Hi [name],

Ready for your notes on V[n]. To turn these around fast and cleanly,
please send everything in one message:

• Merge feedback from everyone into a single list. If two notes
  conflict, tell me which one wins.
• Add timestamps — "at 0:08 the logo feels slow."
• Describe the problem, not the fix — "the intro drags" beats "make
  it faster," because it lets me solve it the right way.
• Mark must-haves vs nice-to-haves.

I'll address the full set in this round. Notes arriving separately or
later may roll into the next round. Thanks!
```

## 2. Change-order email (new request)

```
Subject: [project] — quick note on the new request

Hi [name],

Happy to help with [request] — flagging it sits outside the approved
brief/storyboard, so it's a change rather than a revision.

  • Work involved: [scope]
  • Cost: [amount / day estimate]
  • Timeline impact: [+X days / new delivery date]

Reply "approved" to go ahead and I'll update the schedule, or we keep
to the original plan and revisit later — both are fine. Just let me
know which you'd prefer.
```

## 3. Extra-revision-round notice

```
Subject: [project] — revision rounds

Hi [name],

We've now completed the [2] revision rounds included in scope, and
the latest notes would be a [3rd] round. Glad to do it — additional
rounds run at [rate], roughly [+X days] on the schedule.

Want me to proceed on that basis, or shall we lock the current
version as final? Happy either way.
```

## 4. Stalled-approval nudge

```
Subject: [project] — waiting on your sign-off

Hi [name],

We're paused at the [storyboard] gate waiting on your approval before
the next stage can start. Our delivery date of [date] assumes sign-off
by [date]; each day of delay shifts delivery by the same amount.

A quick "approved" or your consolidated notes is all I need to keep us
on track. Thanks!
```

## 5. Scope-creep pattern reset (when many "tiny" asks have accumulated)

```
Subject: [project] — let's regroup on scope

Hi [name],

A few additions have come in since we locked the brief — each small on
its own, but together they've grown the project beyond the original
scope. So we stay on time and budget, let me bundle them:

  • In original scope (no change): [list]
  • New requests (change order): [list] — [cost] / [+X days]

Tell me which new items to include and I'll send an updated schedule.
Anything we drop, we can pick up in a later phase.
```

Tone rules across all: collaborative, specific, always offer a choice, always state cost and timeline impact, never accuse.
