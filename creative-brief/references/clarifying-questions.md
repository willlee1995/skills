# Clarifying Questions Bank

Send-ready questions, grouped by the 11 dimensions. Pick only the ones with gaps, and combine into one consolidated email (template at the bottom). Never send these one at a time over multiple days.

## 1. Business goal
- "What needs to be different after this goes live — more sign-ups, more sales, fewer support tickets, better recognition?"
- "If the project worked perfectly, what number on a dashboard would move?"
- "Is this about getting attention (top of funnel) or driving an action (bottom of funnel)?"

## 2. Audience
- "Who specifically is this for — and what do they currently believe or feel about you?"
- "What is the one objection in their head we need to overcome?"
- "Where are they when they see this — phone on mute while scrolling, or a presentation on a big screen?"

## 3. Core message
- "If the viewer forgets everything except one sentence, which sentence?"
- "We can land one idea well or three ideas poorly — which single idea wins?"

## 4. Tone / feel
- "Pick 3-5 words for how it should feel."
- "Now the important one: what should it absolutely NOT feel like?"
- "Closer to A (calm, premium, slow, restrained) or B (punchy, loud, fast, playful)?"

## 5. Mandatories
- "Send the logo files, brand guidelines, fonts, and colour codes."
- "Is there any legal text, disclaimer, or super that must appear, and for how long?"
- "Are there regulatory or clearance requirements (e.g. broadcast, financial, pharma)?"
- "How must the logo appear — endcard only, or persistent? Any animation rules?"

## 6. Deliverables
- "Exactly how many videos, at what durations?"
- "Which versions — hero plus cutdowns? Subtitled and clean? Square, vertical, wide?"
- "Do you need a static end-frame or thumbnail as well?"

## 7. Distribution
- "Where exactly will this run — Instagram Reels, paid YouTube pre-roll, website hero, trade-show loop, TV?"
- "Paid or organic? Sound-on or sound-off environment?"

## 8. References
- "Send 2-4 examples you love and one line on what to steal from each."
- "Send one example you hate, so we avoid it."

## 9. Timeline
- "What is the hard air/launch date?"
- "Are there fixed review days when stakeholders are available?"
- "How many people need to approve, and who has the final yes?"

## 10. Budget
- "What budget band are we working in? Projects like this usually land between [X] and [Y] — which end?"
- "Is this a one-off or the first of a series (affects per-unit cost)?"

## 11. Success metric
- "30 days after launch, how will you decide this was worth it?"
- "What target makes this a win — a view count, a conversion rate, internal sign-off?"

## Consolidated intake email template

```
Subject: A few quick answers so we can lock the brief for [project]

Hi [name],

Loved getting this brief — to make sure we nail it first time (and avoid
revision churn later), could you answer the following? A sentence each is
plenty:

1. Goal: what should be different after launch?
2. Audience: who exactly, and what's their main objection?
3. One message: the single line they must remember?
4. Tone: 3-5 words for the feel — and what it must NOT feel like?
5. Mandatories: logo/brand files, plus any legal text that must appear?
6. Deliverables: how many videos, what durations/formats?
7. Where it runs: exact placements (Reels, YouTube, web, TV...)?
8. References: 2-3 you love (with a note why) + 1 you hate?
9. Date: hard launch date, and who gives final sign-off?
10. Budget: rough band we're working in?

As soon as these land, I'll send a one-page brief for your sign-off and
we're off. Thanks!

[name]
```
