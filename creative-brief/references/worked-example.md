# Worked Example

## The raw ask (real-world vague)

> "Hey, we're launching our new app next month and need a cool animated video for social. Something modern and eye-catching that shows what it does. Keep it short. Budget's not huge. Can you send something over?"

## Gaps identified

Concrete: launch timing (vague "next month"), placement ("social"), duration ("short"), budget signal ("not huge").
Missing: business goal, audience, single message, tone guardrail, mandatories, exact deliverables, exact placements, references, hard date, budget number, success metric.

## Consolidated questions sent back

Used the intake email template, asking the 10 grouped questions. Client replied:

1. Goal: drive app installs from the launch campaign.
2. Audience: busy freelancers, 25-40, sceptical of "another productivity app."
3. Message: "Invoicing that takes 30 seconds."
4. Tone: friendly, confident, fast — not corporate, not childish.
5. Mandatories: logo endcard, brand teal #0FB8A8, app-store badges, "Free for 14 days" super.
6. Deliverables: 1× 15s hero + 1× 6s cutdown.
7. Placements: Instagram Reels + TikTok (paid + organic), both 9:16, sound-off default.
8. References: two sent (liked the snappy UI demo pacing of one); anti-ref: a slow corporate explainer.
9. Date: hard launch 14 July; final files needed 10 July; CMO has final sign-off.
10. Budget: band landed at the lower-mid end after anchoring.

## Resulting completed brief

```
PROJECT BRIEF — QuickInvoice Launch          Date: 2026-06-22  Version: 1
Client: QuickInvoice   Decision-maker: CMO   Prepared by: [studio]

1. BUSINESS GOAL
   Drive app installs during the 14 Jul launch push.

2. AUDIENCE
   Freelancers 25-40, time-poor, sceptical of "yet another app."
   Objection: "I don't have time to learn new software."

3. CORE MESSAGE
   Invoicing that takes 30 seconds.

4. TONE / FEEL
   Friendly, confident, fast — but NOT corporate, NOT childish.

5. MANDATORIES
   Logo endcard; brand teal #0FB8A8; App Store + Play badges;
   "Free for 14 days" super held min 2s. No regulatory needs.

6. DELIVERABLES
   1× 15s hero (9:16), 1× 6s cutdown (9:16), 1× static end-frame.

7. DISTRIBUTION
   Instagram Reels + TikTok, paid + organic, sound-off default
   (captions burned in).

8. REFERENCES
   • [link A] — take: snappy UI-demo pacing, <1s holds.
   • Anti-ref: [link B] — avoid: slow corporate VO explainer.

9. TIMELINE (back from 14 Jul launch)
   Air 14 Jul · Final files 10 Jul · Rev rounds 7-9 Jul ·
   Animation 1-6 Jul · Boards 26-30 Jun · Kickoff 23 Jun.

10. BUDGET
    [agreed band, lower-mid] — covers concept, board, 2 deliverables,
    2 revision rounds, end-frame.

11. SUCCESS METRIC
    30 days out: cost-per-install at or below target on Reels/TikTok.

ASSUMPTIONS TO CONFIRM
   • [ASSUMPTION] No voiceover — UI demo + captions only. Confirm.
   • [ASSUMPTION] Stock UI screens provided by client by 25 Jun.

SIGN-OFF
   Approved by: __________  Title: CMO  Date: ______
```

## What the process fixed

- "Cool/modern" became a concrete pacing reference plus a tone guardrail.
- "Short" became 15s + 6s with exact ratios.
- "Social" became two named platforms with sound-off captioning, which sets the spec.
- "Budget not huge" became an agreed band, scoping the number of revision rounds before design started.
- The single message replaced a likely three-message draft, which is the biggest pacing win.
